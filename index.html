Introduction

The given datasets consist of two publicly available sets: "Three Decades of New York Times Headlines", and "New York Times Articles 1920-2020". The first dataset is used as training data for an NLP model that inputs article headlines and predicts news desk classifications. After training on the first dataset, the model is tested on the articles from 1920-2020.

The "Three Decades of New York Times Headlines" dataset provides article headlines, word counts, and news desk information from 1990 to 2020. The "New York Times Articles 1920-2020" dataset includes articles published each year, with titles and excerpts.

In the EDA dashboard, we focus on the top five most common news desks: "Business", "Other", "Metro", "Sports", and "Opinion". You can interact with the charts to explore article distributions by year and discover how word counts evolved over time.
